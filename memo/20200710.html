Problem

Find sets of \(a, b, c, d \in {\rm I\!N} \) which satisfy the following relation $$ \frac{a}{b} + \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a+c}{b+d} $$

Let us first assume that there's any set which satisfies the equation. If we solve the equation for a parameter, say \( b \), the equation reduces to \begin{eqnarray} \frac{ad+cb}{bd} &=& \frac{a+c}{b+d} \\ (ad+cb)(b+d) &=& (a+c)bd \\ ad^2 + cb^2 &=& 0 \\ b^2 &=& - \frac{a}{c} d^2 \end{eqnarray} Therefore, the variable \(b \) can take the two values, \begin{equation} b = \pm i \sqrt{\frac{a}{c}} d \end{equation} which contradicts our first assumption since the right hand side always gives imaginary value while the left hand side has to be real.

Conclusion

As long as we consider in \({\rm I\!N}\) space, there's no solutions for $$ \frac{a}{b} + \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a+c}{b+d} $$ to be hold.
However, if we consider the case which variables are in \({\rm I\!R}\) space, there exist solutions for \begin{equation} b = \pm i \sqrt{\frac{a}{c}} d \end{equation} The left hand side is again, a real number. The right hand side has an imaginary unit, however, the square root part can also give an imaginary unit since \(a\) and \(c\) are in \({\rm I\!R}\).
